The Solution — Super Simple Version
Precursa is like a smoke detector + fire extinguisher + insurance agent — all in one — for your supply chain.
Here's what it does in plain English:
Step 1 — It watches everything, always.
Precursa pulls in live data from weather APIs, port congestion feeds, GPS trackers on carrier ships, customs clearance systems, and geopolitical risk indexes — all at the same time, 24/7. No human is doing this. The system is.
Step 2 — It gives every shipment a Risk Score.
Every shipment gets a number called the DRI — Disruption Risk Index — from 0 to 100. Think of it like a health score for your shipment. Green means fine. Yellow means watch it. Red means something is about to go wrong. This score updates every 2 minutes.
Step 3 — It predicts trouble before it happens.
Using AI, Precursa doesn't wait for the port to be congested. It sees the early warning signs — unusual vessel clustering, weather patterns building, customs slowdowns — and raises the alarm hours before impact. Instead of 4–6 hours to detect, it catches it in under 2 minutes.
Step 4 — It fixes it by itself.
This is the key part. When a shipment's risk score goes critical, Precursa doesn't just send an alert and wait for a human. It automatically calculates alternate routes, picks the best one based on cost, time, and carbon footprint, books the new carrier, updates the warehouse slot, and sends the customer a revised delivery time — all without anyone pressing a button.
Step 5 — It explains every decision.
An AI Copilot (powered by Claude) sits inside the dashboard. After every reroute, it explains in plain English exactly what happened and why. Operators can ask it questions live — "Why was Colombo chosen over Jebel Ali?" — and get a clear, data-backed answer.
